NPN Transistor Action:

  • Current flows from Collector (C) to Emitter (E).
  • The arrow on the circuit symbol Points OUT (Not Pointing iN).
  • Needs a positive base voltage ($V_{BE} \ge 0.7\text{V}$) relative to emitter to turn ON.

PNP Transistor Action:

  • Current flows from Emitter (E) to Collector (C).
  • The arrow on the circuit symbol Points IN (Pointing iN).
  • Needs base to be negative (0V / connected to ground) relative to emitter to activate.

The Amplification Rule

In a current amplifier circuit, a very small base current ($I_B$) controls and modulates a much larger collector current ($I_C$).

Current Gain Formula
$\beta = \frac{I_C}{I_B}$
Where $\beta$ is the amplification factor (Current Gain).
